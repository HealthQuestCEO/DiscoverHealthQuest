😔 The Joke That Went Too Far

You’re Jayden. You like making your friends laugh. At lunch, you make a joke about Noah’s shoes. Everyone laughs—even Noah smiles a little.

But later, you see Noah sitting alone, looking sad.

What do you do?

You walk over and ask, “Hey, are you okay?” Noah shrugs. “It’s fine,” he says. But he doesn’t smile.

You walk past. Maybe he’s just having a bad day. But something about his face makes you pause.

You make another joke about Noah. The group laughs, but this time, someone says, “That’s kinda mean, Jayden.”

You feel your face get hot.

You say, “I’m sorry. I didn’t mean to hurt your feelings.” Noah looks surprised. “Thanks,” he says.

You sit down next to Noah and just hang out for a bit. You don’t talk much, but he seems a little happier.

You keep joking. Everyone laughs—except one friend, who walks away. You wonder if you went too far.

You say, “I didn’t mean to be mean.” Your friend nods. “Just think about it.” You feel unsure—but maybe you can fix this.

Reflection

Sometimes we joke without meaning to hurt someone. But if someone feels hurt, we can choose to make things better.

Reflection

You didn’t talk to Noah. Maybe he’s okay… or maybe he’s not. Next time, you could check in. You never know how much it might help.

Reflection

You made a kind choice. Even small things—like sitting near someone—can help.
